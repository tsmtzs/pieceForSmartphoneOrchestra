About

Piece for smartphone orchestra is a musical work of indeterminate duration for any number of players. It is based on a text score. Can be regarded as a potential aural game on complex systems, beat tones and otoacoustic emission effects.

Players use the smartphone to play a musical instrument. This consists of three buttons and a horizontal bar. A tone starts to play when a button is pressed. It stops when the button is pressed again. At any moment, at most one tone can be heard from a single device. There are three different tones in total. Horizontal bar depicts deviation from the center frequency of a playing tone. Deviation is reflected on the position of a small moving rectangle inside it. When the moving rectangle lies on the center of the bar, deviation is zero.

The instrument is in neutral position, when the screen faces upwards and frequency deviation is zero. In this position the screen defines a horizontal plane. The player can rotate the device around a line on the horizontal plane. Rotation angles can take values from 0 degrees, in neutral position, to 180 degrees. This rotation sets the amplitude of the selected tone. At neutral position no sound should be heard, even if a tone button is pressed.

Define two perpendicular axes y'y, z'z that intersect at the center of the screen, such that y'y lies on the horizontal plane and points to the center of the horizontal bar. Rotating the device around the z'z axis affects the frequency of a playing tone. Rotation angle from 0 to ± 180 degrees deviates the frequency of a selected tone from 0 to ± 100 cents, respectively.

The instrument should be thought of as having a very fragile nature. This should be reflected on the gestures of the player: all rotations and movements must be slow-paced. Furthermore, a tone always starts and stops with the screen facing upwards.

In order to enable audio, the user should make a gesture on the page. A simple tap is adequate. After this, the tone buttons will change color signifying that the device is ready to play.
