About the instrument

Piece for smartphone orchestra uses the smartphone as a musical instrument. The player can play a tone by pressing a button. The tone stops when the button is pressed again. At any moment, only one tone can be heard from a single device. There are three different tones available.

The instrument is in a neutral position, when the screen faces upwards and the title of the piece on the page points forward. In this way two axis are defined: one horizontal and one vertical. The player can rotate the device around the horizontal axis from 0 degrees, in neutral position, to 180 degrees, when the screen points downwards. This rotation sets the amplitude of a selected tone. Rotating around the vertical axis from 0 to 180 degrees deviates the frequency of a selected tone from 100 cents to -100 cents, respectively.

At neutral position no sound should be heard, even if a tone button is pressed. This position might not be the same for all devices. The player is expected to find the exact position on his device, where no sound is heard and the frequency deviation is 0 cents.

The instrument should be thought of as having a very fragile nature. This should be reflected on the gestures of the player: all rotations and movements must be slow-paced. Furthermore, a tone starts and stops -by pressing it's button- at neutral position.

In order to enable audio, the user should make a gesture on the page. A simple tap somewhere on the page will work fine for this.
